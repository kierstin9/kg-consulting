Kierstin Gray Consulting utilizes a proprietary two-week methodology that gets to the heart of a business challenge:

discovery

Lay of the Land

What's working? What's not? What's in our favor? What's in our way?

1
experiments

Micro-shifts

Introduce small behavioural, procedural, or executional modifications to the effort, observing and revising to find the optimal tempo of work.

2
refinement

Tuning

Getting to a team's peak efficiency requires a bit of tuning here and there to get everyone riding the same wave. This is the most important part for setting up for success.

3
managing change

Macro-shifts

With the team in sync, introduce steady and increasing magnitude of change and speed until reaching the team's OPM (Optimal Performance Momentum).

4
transformation

Ritualize. Reflect. Renew. Repeat.

To internalize transformative change, you have to commit to the philosophy not just the task. That means creating authentic rituals around how you work best, with time to reflect on which steps of the method need renewal (modifications) or repeating.

5
finding success

Optimal Performance Momentum

  • The work is challenging but not crippling
  • The milestones pressing but not oppressive
  • The effort is intense but not intimidating
  • The individuals are motivated but not manic
